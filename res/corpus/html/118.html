UFC (Ultimate Fighting Championship) Tickets

UFC Tour Dates

The mixed martial arts (MMA) company Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) is the single largest MMA promoter in the world and features top-ranked fighters from all over the globe. The identity of the UFC rests with numerous fighting styles, such as boxing, Sambo, wrestling, Muay Thai, karate and Brazilian jiu-jitsu.

The UFC tours in states such as New York and Nevada, with performances in the UK and Canada as well. The UFC tour doesn’t just surround one show; instead, it’s a variety of shows that can feature weigh ins, fights, finales and more. With over 300 UFC events and TV deals in the US, Europe, Australia, the Middle East, Asia and more, this MMA company is one of the most profitable with media coverage, popularity and mainstream attention.


About UFC

Formed in 1993, UFC wasn’t always popular. With only 15,000 people on average watching UFC via pay-per-view, it wasn’t until 2005 that the company saw a sharp increase in viewership, which can be linked to the reality TV show The Ultimate Fighter – where up-and-coming fighters are pitted against one another for the chance at a UFC contract. After the surprise success of The Ultimate Fighter, top UFC athletes were soon promoted on the covers of Sports Illustrated and ESPN The Magazine. Now, steady with over a million viewers per show, UFC has acquired World Extreme Cagefighting (WEC), a FOX partnership, a Women’s MMA expansion, international broadcasting and more.


UFC Notable Fighters

There are many notable fighters in the UFC hall of fame, including a list of records held by each athlete.

The youngest lightweight champion, Jon Jones, held the UFC title for 242 consecutive days at age 23. Dubbed one of the greatest young fighters of all time by ESPN and multiple media outlets, he also holds the record for most wins, most successful title defensives, longest win streak and most submissive victories.

Anderson Silva, one of the most popular middleweight UFC champions, will go down in history as holding the longest title streak in the company’s history, as well as most finishes, most consecutive title defensives and more. The UFC President himself, Dana White, has called Silva among the greatest athletes of all time.

In the women’s division, Ronda Rousey is the only fighter to hold a UFC records spot. Awarded the fastest title fight submission, she has also won 12 consecutive MMA fights, losing only one. Rousey was voted Best Female Athlete by ESPN and is UFC’s highest paid fighter – male or female.

Additional notable fighters include Royce Gracie, Randy Couture, Nate Diaz, Conor McGregor, Frankie Edgar, Brock Lesnar, Oleg Taktarov and more.


UFC in Pop Culture

With their own video game developer, UFC has a number of games dedicated to the sport, including UFC: Tapout, Throwdown, Sudden Impact, Personal Trainer and Undisputed 3. The company has also been featured in EA games.

Aside from video games, UFC has a number of collectible action figures dedicated to popular fighters, such as their line of Round 5, which include Tito Ortiz, Randy Couture and Anderson Silva. Their Jakks Pacific collectibles include 8 series releases, featuring a variety of fighters, including Chuck Liddell, Matt Hamill, Forrest Griffin and Matt Hughes.
