2020 NBA Finals Champion Odds Updated: Clippers in Front, Lakers and Bucks Trail

Updating the 2020 NBA champion odds.

  • SHARE

AP Photo/Alex Gallardo

Dick Mahoney

Published on November 01, 2019 at 8:35 AM EDT

Updated on November 06, 2019 at 9:05 AM EST

One week into the NBA season, only two undefeated teams remain: The Philadelphia 76ers (4-0) and the San Antonio Spurs (3-0). However, neither of those teams are in the top three for the NBA betting odds. The 76ers are close to the top, but still half-way to the odds of the Clippers. If we go with the oddmakers, Clippers will be the team to shake our hoops this season.

The Los Angeles Clippers dropped to 3-2 with their second loss in their last three games, a 110-96 defeat in Salt Lake City, where the Jazz’s newly acquired point guard Mike Conley ended a brief shooting skid with a 29-point game, his highest point total as a member of the Jazz. Despite the loss, which was perhaps attributed to a couple injuries that kept Paul George and Kawhi Leonard out of the game, the Clippers remain the favorite to win the 2020 NBA Championship across three different oddsmakers.

The Clippers are not only seeking their first-ever NBA title, they’re seeing their first-ever conference title and first division title since 2014. They won at least 50 games in five consecutive seasons from 2012-13 through 2016-17 but averaged just 45 wins over the last two seasons before acquiring George and Leonard, which shot them to the top of title odds for the first time in franchise history.

The Clippers are followed closely by the Los Angeles Lakers, who won their third straight game on Tuesday night, a 29-point demolition of the Memphis Grizzlies, and are one of five teams in the Western Conference with a 3-1 record in this young season. The Lakers have yet to lose a home game; they’re a perfect 3-0 at the Staples Center, but are just 0-1 on the road. They have three straight road games starting Friday in Dallas.

Behind the Lakers in the NBA Championship Odds are the Milwaukee Bucks, who have been a trendy pick to win their first NBA title after a division title last season, their first since 2001 and only their second in the last 33 years. The Bucks are just 2-2 this season and in a jumbled Eastern Conference but still have the third-best title odds.

Also in the top 10 of NBA Championship Odds: The aforementioned Philadelphia 76ers, who saw Joel Embiid ejected during Wednesday’s win over the Minnesota Timberwolves and could be without their star center for an unknown number of games as he potentially faces suspension for his role in a fight with Timberwolves’ center Karl-Anthony Towns. The 76ers have the fourth-best title odds, followed by Houston Rockets, Golden State Warriors, and the aforementioned Utah Jazz.

The Warriors still rank in the top six despite the free-agency departure of Kevin Durant to the Brooklyn Nets and Klay Thompson’s continued recovery from a knee injury he suffered in last year’s NBA Finals. The Warriors were heavy NBA title favorites in each of the last three seasons but have dropped to sixth with a rebuilt roster.
